<h2>Japanese Learning Sentences</h2>

<h3>N5 Level</h3>
<p><strong style="color:blue;">Original:</strong> 粉雪が空から優しく降りてくる<br>
*Translation*: The powdery snow gently falls from the sky.</p>
<p><strong style="color:blue;">New Sentence (Use of "空から" - "from the sky"):</strong> 太陽が空から輝いている。<br>
*Translation*: The sun is shining down from the sky.</p>

<p><strong style="color:blue;">Original:</strong> あなたは立ち止まり<br>
*Translation*: You stop in your tracks.</p>
<p><strong style="color:blue;">New Sentence (Use of "立ち止まる" - "to stop"):</strong> 私は信号で立ち止まりました。<br>
*Translation*: I stopped at the traffic light.</p>

<p><strong style="color:blue;">Original:</strong> I still love you<br>
*Translation*: I still love you.</p>
<p><strong style="color:blue;">New Sentence (Use of "I still..." - expressing ongoing feeling):</strong> I still remember you.<br>
*Translation*: I still remember you.</p>

<h3>N4 Level</h3>
<p><strong style="color:blue;">Original:</strong> 思い出していますか 空を見上げながら<br>
*Translation*: Do you remember as you look up at the sky?</p>
<p><strong style="color:blue;">New Sentence (Use of "ながら" - "while doing something"):</strong> 音楽を聴きながら本を読んでいます。<br>
*Translation*: I’m reading a book while listening to music.</p>

<p><strong style="color:blue;">Original:</strong> 雪が切ない<br>
*Translation*: The snow feels bittersweet.</p>
<p><strong style="color:blue;">New Sentence (Use of "切ない" - "bittersweet"):</strong> 映画の結末が切なくて涙が出た。<br>
*Translation*: The ending of the movie was bittersweet and made me cry.</p>

<h3>N3 Level</h3>
<p><strong style="color:blue;">Original:</strong> いとおしく見えた<br>
*Translation*: You looked so dear to me.</p>
<p><strong style="color:blue;">New Sentence (Use of "いとおしい" - "dear, precious"):</strong> 赤ちゃんがとてもいとおしく感じられた。<br>
*Translation*: The baby felt so precious to me.</p>

<p><strong style="color:blue;">Original:</strong> あなたが楽しそうに話をしてくれた<br>
*Translation*: You happily talked with me.</p>
<p><strong style="color:blue;">New Sentence (Use of "楽しそうに" - "happily"):</strong> 友達が楽しそうに新しい仕事について話してくれた。<br>
*Translation*: My friend happily talked about their new job.</p>

<p><strong style="color:blue;">Original:</strong> 初めて触れた唇の温もりも忘れない<br>
*Translation*: I won’t forget the warmth of the first touch of your lips.</p>
<p><strong style="color:blue;">New Sentence (Use of "初めて" - "first time"):</strong> 初めて会った日のことを今も覚えています。<br>
*Translation*: I still remember the day we first met.</p>

<p><strong style="color:blue;">Original:</strong> あの日見た白い世界<br>
*Translation*: The white world I saw that day.</p>
<p><strong style="color:blue;">New Sentence (Use of "あの日見た" - "the day I saw"): </strong>旅行中に見た美しい景色が忘れられません。<br>
*Translation*: I can’t forget the beautiful scenery I saw during the trip.</p>

<h3>N2 Level</h3>
<p><strong style="color:blue;">Original:</strong> あの時触れた指先の冷たさも忘れない<br>
*Translation*: I won’t forget the coldness of your fingertips that I felt that time.</p>
<p><strong style="color:blue;">New Sentence (Use of "忘れない" - "not to forget"): </strong>過去の辛い経験を忘れないことで強くなれる。<br>
*Translation*: Remembering past painful experiences can make you stronger.</p>
